Why most launches miss

Traditional central‑location tests miss the kitchen. That’s where decisions happen. Without in‑home behavior data, teams over‑index on stated opinions—and launches fail far too often. You shouldn’t bet your budget on guesswork.

Meet your guide

We know the pressure on product leads—timelines shrink; decisions don’t. Our team blends sensory science and computer vision to turn real‑life eating into clear, quantitative signals you can act on.

In‑home behavior, not lab guesses

Computer vision captures bites, chews, sips, utensil use, and dwell time, while our app masks faces for privacy.

Emotions & context, quantified

Moment‑by‑moment emotions, audio interpretation, and activity signals tie reactions to specific prep and eating moments.

Classic KPIs included

Liking, acceptance, purchase intent, and competitor comparisons—together in one view.

How it works

You prepare your food samples—we handle the rest. Eatpol ships to testers with a QR code. Testers cook and eat while recording; privacy is ensured with automatic face masking.

  • Traditional product questionnaire results (liking, taste, purchase intent)
  • Emotion detection, activity tracking
  • Bite & chew counts for eating‑behavior insights
  • AI‑interpreted audio feedback for nuance beyond surveys

FAQs

How is privacy handled?

Faces are auto‑masked in videos; we store de‑identified signals only and comply with GDPR. A Data Processing Agreement (DPA) is available on request.

Do we need special hardware?

No—testers use their own phones. We provide simple setup guidance inside the app.

How fast are results?

Typical studies deliver a report within 7-10 days after the last test is performed. Timelines depend on audience and logistics.

Can we compare against competitors?

Yes—include one or multiple competitor SKUs. We report relative performance and key drivers.

What if samples require cooking?

We capture preparation steps to connect outcomes with real‑world usage and identify friction.
