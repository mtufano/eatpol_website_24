Why most product launches fail

85% of new food products fail within 2 years (Forbes). Not because the products are bad—but because they were tested in the wrong place, with the wrong tools.

Sensory booths are sterile rooms where participants “evaluate”—not eat. They aren't cooking for their family or deciding what to buy. They circle a number on a questionnaire, collect their payment, and leave.

That 1–10 taste score? It tells you what people rate. Not what they buy. Not what they feel. Not why they reach for your competitor’s product instead.

Real food decisions happen in kitchens, with emotions, in chaos. If you aren't testing there, you aren't really testing.

FAQs

How is privacy handled?

Faces are auto-masked in videos; we store de-identified signals only and comply with GDPR. A Data Processing Agreement (DPA) is available on request.

Do we need special hardware?

No — testers use their own phones. We provide simple setup guidance inside the app.

How fast are results?

From kickoff to results in 2 weeks. Consumer interviews (Vox) can be completed in as little as 2 days. In-home product testing (Domus) runs in parallel. Results appear in your Eatpol Studio dashboard as soon as testing wraps.

Can we compare against competitors?

Yes — include one or multiple competitor SKUs. We report relative performance and key drivers.

What if samples require cooking?

We capture preparation steps to connect outcomes with real-world usage and identify friction.
